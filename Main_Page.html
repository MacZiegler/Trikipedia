From today's featured article

The Missouri State University chapter of the Advocating for Families and Children community group has a series of stated purposes listed on their campus web page. These include:

  • To promote and maintain high standards for the care and education of young children;
  • To promote the citizens of the region to take action which promotes the welfare of young children and their families;
  • To encourage continuing study of the needs of young children and their families, interpretation of such findings and acceptance of these by such individuals and groups;
  • To cooperate with other rganizations and disciplines for the improvement of services to young children and their families in the region, the state, the nation and the world;
  • To keep abreast of and participate in the development of research and trends in early childhood education, family dynamics and related fields.
  • To promote opportunities for students to learn about early care and education initiatives, programs and community efforts.

Did you know...

  • ... that the goal of this exercise is to rebuild how the site looks not how it behaves, so the links don't need to link anywhere.
  • ... that to make a dummy link, use the # as the value of the href attribute on your link. For example: <a href=”#”>.
  • ... that you should make sure you are committing your changes appropriately and pushing to a remote repository on GitHub.
  • ... that you should not rush through this. Being able to accurately rebuild a site is a great skill to have and will really help in understanding how CSS works to style your pages.

    From today's featured list

    This has been an interesting exercise. The Wikipedia main page is not built on Bootstrap, so getting close enough to the main page exact layout is a challenge. In building the page Bootstrap style, I chose not to use the exact techniques employed by Wikipedia. One of my goals for this page was responsiveness; I wanted the page to transition smoothly from small mobile screen to giant monitor. Unsurprisingly, this turns out to be far easier said than done.

    I wanted to use the page for more than just a class assignment, so I renamed the project Trikipedia, then created new article material and new graphics. It rather feels like an exercise in replicating the master paintings of classic art. The analogy holds longer than one might expect, since using Bootstrap to build a Wikipedia page seems much like repainting the classic oil painting using modern acrylics. Doable, but different.
